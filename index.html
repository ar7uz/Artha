— I The studio

Opened in 2017. Run by the same teachers, still.

Artha is a quiet room. That is the whole brief.

We opened in 2017 in a single white-plaster room on Mukimiy 1st Drive — at the time, the only place in Yakkasaray where a serious yoga student could practise hatha at seven in the morning, sit a sound meditation in the same room at noon, and have a vegetarian lunch in the next room at one.

Eight years later we are six rooms — a yoga shala, a meditation room, a sound chamber, a treatment room, a small vegetarian café, and a shop with the things the teachers actually use. Same plaster, same teachers, same patience.

We do not sell quick wins. There is no thirty-day challenge, no ascension package, no metaphysical merchandise at the entrance. We sell a single, repeatable thing: one hour, in a quiet warm room, with a teacher who knows what they are doing.

Our students range from eighteen-year-old students who come at six in the morning before class to seventy-year-old grandmothers who come for slow hatha twice a week. The teachers are the same in both rooms.

If you have practised before, the room will feel familiar. If you have not, book the slow Hatha at eleven on a Saturday and we will take it from there.

— III The space

Six rooms, one warm wing.

A converted ground-floor space on Mukimiy 1st Drive — six warm-plaster rooms, daylight from courtyard windows, no street noise. Below: the actual layout, by room.

— No. 01 · the practice Yoga shala · main The largest room, daylight from two sides, twelve mats with comfortable distance. cap. 12 students
— No. 02 Sound chamber Acoustic-treated, low ceiling, singing bowls and gong on the floor. Sound baths only. cap. 16 sitting
