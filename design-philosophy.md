# Artha Mind · Body · Soul — Design Philosophy

## Movement: **"Breath & Practice"**

A visual language for a Tashkent wellness centre that has, by every measure, earned its position — five-star Yandex average across four hundred and forty-seven reviews, a multi-format practice that runs yoga, sound therapy, vocal and dance therapies, vegetarian café, treatment rooms and a small retail shop, all under one roof on Mukimiy 1st Drive — and yet has no main brand site, only a booking subdomain and an Instagram. The visual reference is not the cliché yoga aesthetic of pastel chakras, lotus-flower mandalas, Sanskrit script and Saraswati silhouettes. The reference is a small modern urban yoga brand — **Sky Ting** in New York, **Y7 Studio** in Los Angeles, **Modo Yoga** internationally — crossed with the wellness issue of *Cereal Magazine*: warm plaster walls, soft natural light, generous whitespace, grounded earth tones, modern editorial type, and a calm kind of confidence that does not need to advertise its spirituality.

Form is **modern wellness editorial**. The page is laid out the way a thoughtful student would want to read about a practice before committing to a membership: who is the founder and why did she open this place, what practices are taught and by whom, what does a class cost, when can I come, and where exactly do I sit when I arrive. Sticky-scroll for the founder's story, a practices catalogue with pricing visible, a clear weekly schedule, an honest map of which room is where, and a single magnetic CTA at the booking moment. Specifics replace mantra-speak. Numbers replace incense.

Colour is **the palette of a real Tashkent courtyard at golden hour** — never the lavender-purple-pastel of a New Age studio, never the saturated jewel tones of a yoga merchandise catalogue. **Warm plaster** (#efe9de) is the page — the off-white of an old whitewashed wall in afternoon light. **Warm dark** (#1f1a14) is the typography — almost black, with a slight brown undertone of polished walnut. **Dusty mauve-rose** (#b8736e) is the brand colour — drawn from the colour of unfired clay pottery, completely distinct from every other warm tone used in this portfolio's previous nine commissions; it is the colour of breath at sunset, of a yoga mat that has been used for six years, of the founder's favourite ceramic cup. **Warm clay** (#c4856b) is a secondary accent, used where the eye should land on a teacher's name or a price. **Soft sage** (#9aa394) appears once or twice as the gentlest support — the green of a healthy houseplant in a warm room. There is no purple, no gold-leaf accent, no chakra rainbow.

Typography pairs three voices, none used in any of the previous nine commissions in this portfolio. **Lora** is the display voice — a contemporary serif designed for screen reading with the warmth of a hand-set magazine title; its italic has the breathing curve of a real exhale. **Sora** is the body voice — a humanist geometric sans designed for friendly modern interfaces, set tight, the kind of sans a thoughtful wellness publication uses for a teacher's biography. **Fira Mono** is reserved for class times, prices, durations and phone numbers — the technical truths of a studio's week, set in tabular figures the way a printed schedule would be set on the studio's counter card.

Geometry is **soft**. Border radii sit at 12-20 pixels — the visual softness that distinguishes a wellness space from a corporate gym. Cards have generous padding, sections breathe at 100-120 pixels of vertical space, and the hero ornament is a **slow-breathing concentric circle field** — the visual rhythm of the four-seven-eight breath, not a static logo. The page exhales as you scroll.

Motion is governed by ui-ux-pro-max rules and tuned to the calm of the practice itself: **150–300 ms** on micro-interactions, **transform/opacity only** (per `transform-performance`), **motion-meaning** (every animation expresses cause-effect, never decoration), **reduced-motion** fully honoured. The Motion library carries: a hero with character-by-character entrance under low-stiffness `spring-physics` (the way a teacher reads a name from the sign-in sheet, slowly); a calm marquee of practice names running across one band; a sticky-scroll founder biography; **subtle three-dimensional tilt** on practice cards that mimics a card lifted gently from a side table; a **magnetic booking CTA** that follows the cursor like a teacher's hand offering a yoga block; a counter that fills as the eye reaches the years-of-practice stat; line-by-line philosophy reveal timed to a slow reading pace, not a marketing-attention pace.

Composition is asymmetric, ruled, generous — and built to serve the actual decision a premium wellness customer makes: *do these people know what they're doing, will I feel welcome, and can I trust my body to them?* Where another wellness site would print *"Find your inner peace!"* with stock yoga photography and a chakra wheel, this one prints *"Eight years. Six practices. One quiet room on Mukimiy."* in soft Lora italic on warm plaster, and lets the specifics replace the sentiment. Numbers replace adjectives. The page is what an eight-year five-star wellness centre should have always had: a warm, calm, modern presence that looks as carefully tended as the practice itself.
